kind}` descriptor, build the adjacency, run the gate (gate-kills are first-class), compute
`χ = n − ν(H)`, then walk the had₂→had₃ tiering — replicating the battery runbook legs DIRECTLY on
the descriptor-built `adj` (exactly as `pool/cdm/adjudicate.py`'s escalation hook does; `run_candidate`
cannot be used because it regenerates from `(family, seed)` and its `_generate` ignores `params`,
and P2 needs the abelian descriptor + RNG contract v2). Classify the outcome as KILLED (g≤0, packs),
SHC-CANDIDATE (g>0 screen), or RESISTANT (E3 queue), compute g, and persist an honest verified g(G)
certificate.

Because CBC and CP-SAT are CO-EQUAL in `differential_verdict` (both must reach PROVED_OPTIMAL to
license AGREED_KILL / SHC_CANDIDATE), BOTH backends here are bounded DETERMINISTICALLY: CP-SAT by
`det_time`, CBC by `det_nodes` (the 08-02 node-cap). A recorded DECIDED/RESISTANT verdict is therefore
a function of (n, seed), never of machine speed — a wall-clock cutoff on either backend is forbidden.

Purpose: turn a generated instance into a stored, verified, honestly-labeled g(G) verdict — the
walking skeleton of the whole hunt, complete after this plan.
Output: `pool/sumfree/{screen,adjudicate}.py`. Turns the remaining test_screen + test_determinism GREEN.
